CHAPTER I GENERAL PROVISIONS

Article 1 (Purpose)
The purpose of these Rules is to provide for matters delegated by the Road Traffic Act and the Enforcement Decree of the same Act and other matters necessary for the enforcement thereof.

Article 2 (Devices and Equipment Excluded from Vehicles and Horses)
(1) The term "strollers, walking aids for the disabled, walkers for the elderly, and other devices and equipment prescribed by Ordinance of the Ministry of the Interior and Safety" in Article 2, subparagraph 10 of the Road Traffic Act (hereinafter referred to as the "Act") means any of the following devices and equipment with a width of one meter or less: <Amended on Oct. 19, 2023>
1. Strollers;
2. Walking aids for the disabled (referring to manual wheelchairs, electric wheelchairs, and medical scooters according to the standards for medical devices determined by the Minister of Food and Drug Safety pursuant to Article 19 of the Medical Devices Act);
3. Walkers for the elderly;
4. Play equipment under Article 11 (3) of the Act (limited to those used by children);
5. Handcarts without power;
6. Motorcycles, motor-driven cycles, or bicycles being moved by a driver who has dismounted and is pulling or carrying it;
7. Devices and equipment used for tasks such as road repair/maintenance and road construction (limited to those not carrying people or transporting cargo).
(2) The term "strollers, walking aids for the disabled, walkers for the elderly, outdoor mobile robots under subparagraph 21-3, and other devices and equipment prescribed by Ordinance of the Ministry of the Interior and Safety" in Article 2, subparagraph 17 (a) 5) of the Act means any of the following devices and equipment: <Newly Inserted on Oct. 19, 2023>
1. Any device or equipment falling under any of the subparagraphs of paragraph (1);
2. Outdoor mobile robots.
[Wholly Amended on Apr. 20, 2022]

Article 3 (Application for Designation of Emergency Vehicles, etc.)
(1) A person or institution, etc. intending to obtain designation as an emergency vehicle pursuant to Article 2, subparagraph 22 (d) of the Act and the proviso of Article 2 (1) of the Enforcement Decree of the Road Traffic Act (hereinafter referred to as the "Decree") shall submit an application for designation of an emergency vehicle in Form 1, attached with the following documents, to the Commissioner of a City/Do Provincial Police Agency: <Amended on Nov. 27, 2009; Dec. 31, 2010; Dec. 9, 2011; Dec. 31, 2020>
1. One copy of the lease agreement (limited to cases where the vehicle is owned by another person);
2. Two photographs of the vehicle to be designated.
(2) When the Commissioner of a City/Do Provincial Police Agency designates an emergency vehicle upon an application under paragraph (1), he/she shall issue a certificate of designation of an emergency vehicle in Form 2 to the applicant. <Amended on Dec. 31, 2020>
(3) The certificate of designation of an emergency vehicle issued pursuant to paragraph (2) shall be attached to a visible place on the front windshield of the relevant vehicle.
(4) If a certificate of designation of an emergency vehicle is lost or worn out and unusable, an application for re-issuance of a certificate of designation of an emergency vehicle in Form 3 shall be submitted to the Commissioner of a City/Do Provincial Police Agency for re-issuance. Provided, That when applying for re-issuance due to the certificate being worn out and unusable, the worn-out certificate shall be attached to the application. <Amended on Dec. 31, 2020>
(5) The Commissioner of a City/Do Provincial Police Agency who receives documents pursuant to paragraphs (1) and (4) shall confirm the applicant's business registration certificate and vehicle registration certificate through the joint use of administrative information under Article 36 (1) of the Electronic Government Act, and if the applicant does not consent to the confirmation, he/she shall have the applicant attach copies thereof. <Newly Inserted on Nov. 27, 2009; Sep. 10, 2010; Dec. 31, 2020>

Article 3-2 (Alcohol-Ignition Interlocks)

When the Commissioner of a City/Do Provincial Police Agency intends to install a crosswalk pursuant to Article 10 (1) of the Act, it shall comply with the following standards: <Amended on Apr. 27, 2007; Apr. 26, 2012; Nov. 29, 2016; Dec. 31, 2020; Oct. 20, 2022>

Article 28 (Types of Drugs Prohibited for Driving)
Drugs that prohibit a driver of a motor vehicle, etc. (excluding personal mobility devices) or a tram from driving due to their influence pursuant to Article 45 of the Act shall be hallucinogenic substances under Article 11 of the Enforcement Decree of the Chemical Substances Control Act, which are hazardous chemical substances that cause excitement, hallucination, or anesthesia. <Amended on Nov. 27, 2009; Dec. 24, 2014; Mar. 28, 2019; Dec. 10, 2020>

Article 28-2 (Method and Procedure for Measuring Drug Use)
(1) The term "methods prescribed by Ordinance of the Ministry of the Interior and Safety, such as a simple saliva reagent test" in Article 45 (2) of the Act means any of the following methods:
1. Assessment of walking and standing conditions: A method in which a police officer evaluates whether a driver has taken drugs by testing their motor skills, such as straight-line walking, turning, and standing on one foot;
2. Simple reagent test: A method of measuring whether drugs have been taken by collecting saliva, etc. and using a simple reagent kit.
(2) The term "methods prescribed by Ordinance of the Ministry of the Interior and Safety, such as blood collection" in Article 45 (3) of the Act means a method of measuring drug use through component appraisal of samples such as blood or urine by a specialized institution such as the National Forensic Service (hereinafter referred to as "blood/urine test").
(3) When measuring drug use pursuant to paragraphs (1) and (2), the following procedures shall be followed:
1. When assessing walking and standing conditions, the following procedures:
(a) The test shall be conducted when a police officer suspects that a driver has driven while in a state where there is a concern that they cannot drive normally due to the influence of drugs (hereinafter referred to as "drugged driving") based on a comprehensive consideration of the driver's driving behavior, appearance, words, actions, and attitude;
(b) The driver's straight-line walking, turning, and standing on one foot shall be comprehensively tested on-site to assess whether they are in a state where drugged driving is suspected.
2. When conducting a simple reagent test, the following procedures:
(a) The test shall be conducted after assessing the walking and standing conditions under subparagraph 1;
(b) The test shall be conducted by collecting the driver's saliva, etc.
3. When measuring by blood/urine test, the following procedures:
(a) The test shall be conducted when a driver who objects to the result of a simple reagent test consents to measurement by a blood/urine test, or when a driver requests measurement by a blood/urine test before a simple reagent test;
(b) Samples such as blood or urine shall be collected at a medical institution such as a nearby hospital or clinic, and non-alcoholic disinfectant shall be used when collecting blood. Provided, That in the case of urine collection, if the driver consents, it may be performed at a nearby police station, etc., other than a medical institution;
(c) The collected blood or urine, etc. shall be sent to a specialized institution such as the National Forensic Service for appraisal.
(4) Necessary matters concerning the method and procedure for measuring drug use, other than those provided for in paragraphs (1) through (3), shall be determined by the Commissioner General of the Korean National Police Agency.
[This Article Newly Inserted on Apr. 1, 2026]

[Title Amended on Oct. 20, 2022]

Article 31 (Reasons for Not Wearing Seat Belts)
Cases where a person is not required to wear a seat belt or where a driver is not required to ensure a passenger wears a seat belt pursuant to the proviso of Article 50 (1) and the proviso of Article 53 (2) of the Act shall be any of the following cases: <Amended on Dec. 31, 2014; Sep. 28, 2018>
1. When a person for whom wearing a seat belt is deemed inappropriate due to injury, disease, disability, pregnancy, etc. drives or rides in a vehicle;
2. When driving a vehicle in reverse;
3. When a person for whom wearing a seat belt is deemed inappropriate due to height, obesity, or other physical conditions drives or rides in a vehicle;
4. When an emergency vehicle is being operated for its original purpose;
5. When driving or riding in a vehicle being escorted or guided by a police vehicle for protection, etc.;
6. When driving or riding in a vehicle used for national referendum campaigns, election campaigns, and national referendum/election management affairs pursuant to the National Referendum Act and election-related statutes;
7. When a person engaged in duties that require frequent getting on and off, such as mail delivery and waste collection, drives or rides in a vehicle for the relevant duties;
8. When a driver of a vehicle for passenger transport business pursuant to the Passenger Transport Service Act cannot ensure a passenger wears a seat belt due to the passenger's intoxication, drug use, etc., or when a passenger does not wear one despite the driver's guidance.

Article 32 (Personal Protective Equipment)
(1) The term "personal protective equipment prescribed by Ordinance of the Ministry of the Interior and Safety" in Article 50 (3) of the Act means a safety helmet for riding that meets the following standards: <Amended on Mar. 6, 2008; Jul. 9, 2010; Mar. 23, 2013; Nov. 19, 2014; Jul. 26, 2017>
1. It shall have a sufficient field of vision horizontally and vertically;
2. The sunshade or visor shall not obstruct the field of vision due to wind pressure;
3. It shall not significantly impede hearing;
4. It shall have impact absorption and penetration resistance;
5. It shall be able to be fixed so that it does not easily come off due to impact;
6. Its weight shall be 2 kilograms or less;
7. It shall have a structure that does not cause injury to the human body;
8. A reflector shall be attached to the back of the helmet for night operation.
(2) The term "personal protective equipment prescribed by Ordinance of the Ministry of the Interior and Safety" in Article 50 (4) of the Act means a safety helmet that meets the standards under paragraph (1) 1 and subparagraphs 3 through 7 of the same paragraph. <Newly Inserted on Jul. 9, 2010; Mar. 23, 2013; Nov. 19, 2014; Jul. 26, 2017>

Article 33-5 (Registration Procedure for Alcohol-Ignition Interlocks)
(1) A person intending to register an alcohol-ignition interlock pursuant to Article 50-3 (1) of the Act shall submit an application for registration (change) of an alcohol-ignition interlock (for individuals) in Form 17-2 to the Korea Road Traffic Authority under Article 2 of the Korea Road Traffic Authority Act (hereinafter referred to as the "Korea Road Traffic Authority") and present an identification certificate. Provided, That if the person intending to register the alcohol-ignition interlock so desires, identity confirmation may be performed by comparing fingerprint information through an electronic method instead of presenting an identification certificate.
(2) A person intending to register an alcohol-ignition interlock pursuant to Article 50-3 (2) of the Act shall submit an application for registration (change) of an alcohol-ignition interlock (for business operators) in Form 17-3 to the Korea Road Traffic Authority.
(3) When applying for registration of an alcohol-ignition interlock pursuant to paragraphs (1) and (2), documents under subparagraphs 1 through 5 shall be attached. Provided, That the document under subparagraph 5 is required only when applying for registration pursuant to paragraph (2).
1. A certificate of installation of an alcohol-ignition interlock in Form 17-4;
2. A copy of the identification card of the owner of the motor vehicle, etc., and a consent form for registration of an alcohol-ignition interlock in Form 17-5 (only when the owner of the motor vehicle, etc. and the applicant for registration of the alcohol-ignition interlock are different);
3. Any of the following documents:
(a) In the case of a motorcycle: A copy of the certificate of report on use of a motorcycle under Article 48 (1) of the Motor Vehicle Management Act;
(b) In the case of construction machinery: A copy of the certificate of registration of construction machinery under Article 3 (3) of the Construction Machinery Management Act;
(c) In the case of a motor vehicle (excluding motorcycles and construction machinery): A copy of the vehicle registration certificate;
4. A power of attorney and an identification certificate of the agent (only when an agent applies);
5. Documents proving the license, permit, registration, authorization, or report on passenger vehicle transport business pursuant to the Passenger Transport Service Act or truck transport business pursuant to the Trucking Transport Service Act.
(4) The Korea Road Traffic Authority that receives an application under paragraph (1) or (2) shall confirm the applicant's resident registration certificate through the joint use of administrative information under Article 36 (1) of the Electronic Government Act. Provided, That if the applicant does not consent to the confirmation, the Korea Road Traffic Authority shall have the applicant attach the relevant document.
(5) When the Korea Road Traffic Authority deems that an application for registration under paragraph (1) or (2) meets the registration criteria, it shall record the fact in the register of alcohol-ignition interlocks in Form 17-6 and issue a certificate of registration of an alcohol-ignition interlock in Form 17-7.
[This Article Newly Inserted on Dec. 12, 2024]

Article 33-6 (Change of Registration of Alcohol-Ignition Interlocks)
(1) The term "important matters prescribed by Ordinance of the Ministry of the Interior and Safety" in the latter part of Article 50-3 (1) and (2) of the Act means the following matters. Provided, That subparagraph 3 applies only to cases of change of registration pursuant to Article 50-3 (2) of the Act.
1. Matters concerning device indications, such as the type and model of the alcohol-ignition interlock;
2. Matters concerning indications of the motor vehicle, etc. equipped with the alcohol-ignition interlock, such as the type of vehicle and the chassis number;
3. The trade name and the name of the representative of the transport business operator.
(2) A person who has registered an alcohol-ignition interlock pursuant to Article 33-5 (hereinafter referred to as an "alcohol-ignition interlock registrant") shall, if any of the matters under the subparagraphs of paragraph (1) is changed, apply for change of registration to the Commissioner of a City/Do Provincial Police Agency within 14 days from the date the reason occurred. In such cases, Article 33-5 shall apply mutatis mutandis to the procedure for applying for change of registration.

Article 33-7 (Revocation of Registration of Alcohol-Ignition Interlocks)
(1) An alcohol-ignition interlock registrant and the owner of a motor vehicle, etc. equipped with an alcohol-ignition interlock may apply for revocation of registration of the alcohol-ignition interlock to the Korea Road Traffic Authority if there is any of the following reasons. Provided, That in the case of the reason under subparagraph 3, the application for revocation of registration shall be made within 30 days from the date the reason occurred.
1. When the period for attaching the alcohol-ignition interlock pursuant to Article 80-2 (2) of the Act has passed;
2. When the relevant alcohol-ignition interlock is not used;
3. When the alcohol-ignition interlock cannot recover its original function due to a breakdown, damage, etc., or when it is lost or destroyed.
(2) A person intending to apply for revocation of registration pursuant to paragraph (1) shall submit an application for revocation of registration of an alcohol-ignition interlock in Form 17-8, attached with documents proving the reason under any of the subparagraphs of paragraph (1), to the Korea Road Traffic Authority.
(3) The Korea Road Traffic Authority may ex officio revoke the registration of an alcohol-ignition interlock without delay in any of the following cases. Provided, That in the case of the reason under subparagraph 1, the registration shall be revoked.
1. When the registration was made by false or other fraudulent means;
2. When a person who must apply for revocation of registration pursuant to paragraph (1) 3 fails to apply within 30 days from the date the reason occurred;
3. When the period during which the submission of operation records and the inspection for normal operation of the alcohol-ignition interlock pursuant to Article 50-3 (6) of the Act have not been performed exceeds 30 days.
(4) The Korea Road Traffic Authority may issue a notification of revocation of registration of an alcohol-ignition interlock in Form 17-9 if a person who applied for revocation of registration pursuant to paragraph (1) so desires. In such cases, if the alcohol-ignition interlock registrant and the owner of the motor vehicle, etc. equipped with the alcohol-ignition interlock are different, the owner shall be notified of the fact of revocation of registration.
(5) When the Korea Road Traffic Authority has revoked the registration of an alcohol-ignition interlock pursuant to paragraph (3), it shall notify the alcohol-ignition interlock registrant of the fact by a notification of revocation of registration of an alcohol-ignition interlock in Form 17-9. In such cases, if the alcohol-ignition interlock registrant and the owner of the motor vehicle, etc. equipped with the alcohol-ignition interlock are different, the owner shall also be notified.
[This Article Newly Inserted on Dec. 12, 2024]

Article 33-8 (Submission of Operation Records of Alcohol-Ignition Interlocks)
(1) An alcohol-ignition interlock registrant shall, pursuant to Article 50-3 (6) of the Act, submit the operation records recorded in the device every 6 months starting from the day after the last day of the inspection period under Article 33-9 (2). In such cases, if the operation records are submitted twice a year starting from the day after the last day of the inspection period under Article 33-9 (2), it shall be deemed that they have been submitted twice a year pursuant to Article 50-3 (6) of the Act.
(2) The submission of operation records of an alcohol-ignition interlock under paragraph (1) shall be performed by electronically submitting the alcohol-ignition interlock in which the operation records are stored to the Korea Road Traffic Authority when undergoing an inspection for normal operation under Article 33-9.
(3) The Korea Road Traffic Authority shall keep and manage the operation records submitted pursuant to paragraph (2) for 3 years.
[This Article Newly Inserted on Dec. 12, 2024]

Article 33-9 (Timing, Standards, and Method of Inspection for Normal Operation)
(1) An alcohol-ignition interlock registrant shall, pursuant to Article 50-3 (6) of the Act, regularly undergo an inspection to check the normal operation, etc. of the alcohol-ignition interlock (hereinafter referred to as "normal operation inspection") every 6 months starting from any of the following dates:
1. When newly registered: The date of new registration;
2. When a normal operation inspection has been performed within the normal operation inspection period under paragraph (2): The day after the expiration date of the normal operation inspection valid period;
3. When a normal operation inspection has been performed after the normal operation inspection period under paragraph (2) has passed: The day after the date of undergoing the normal operation inspection.
(2) The normal operation inspection period shall be a period within 31 days before and after the expiration date of the normal operation inspection valid period.
(3) The expiration date of the normal operation inspection valid period in paragraph (1) 2 and paragraph (2) shall be the day when 6 months have passed from any of the dates under the subparagraphs of paragraph (1).
(4) The standards and method for normal operation inspection shall be as specified in Table 13-2.
(5) If an alcohol-ignition interlock registrant has undergone a normal operation inspection twice a year starting from any of the dates under the subparagraphs of paragraph (1), it shall be deemed that they have undergone inspections twice a year pursuant to Article 50-3 (6) of the Act.

(c) For large-sized towing vehicle license, small-sized towing vehicle license, and wrecker license:
1) Large-sized towing vehicle license and small-sized towing vehicle license: 1 person per functional training course;

(d) For Class II small-sized license or motor-driven cycle license: 1 person per 50 square meters of the area of the relevant functional training ground.
2. Maximum number of daily educations: 20 times

Article 142 (Unavoidable Reasons)
The term "other unavoidable reasons" in Article 160 (4) 1 of the Act means any of the following cases:
1. When it is for preventing or suppressing crimes or investigating urgent cases/accidents;
2. When it is for road construction or traffic guidance/enforcement;
3. When it is for transporting or treating emergency patients;
4. When it is for rescue operations in case of fire, flood, or disaster;
5. When it is for helping disabled persons get on or off a vehicle pursuant to the Welfare of Persons with Disabilities Act;
6. When there is other reasonable ground to recognize it as an unavoidable reason.

Article 7 (Interim Measures regarding Driver's License Revocation/Suspension Standards, etc.) (1) The amended provisions of Table 28, subparagraphs 2 and 3 shall apply starting from the first violation after the enforcement of these Rules.
(2) When revoking a practice driver's license according to the amended provisions of Table 29, the number of violations shall be calculated starting from the first violation after the enforcement of these Rules.
Article 8 (Interim Measures regarding Forms) (1) Forms according to previous provisions at the time of enforcement of these Rules may be used together with the forms according to these Rules until December 31, 2012, but the columns revised according to these Rules must be corrected before use.
(2) Driver's licenses issued before the enforcement of these Rules shall be deemed issued according to these Rules.
